Understanding Sentiment Charts

Sentiment is displayed as percentage distribution to normalize across different event volumes:

Example Calculation:

Day 1: 50 events analyzed
‚Üí 15 Positive (30%) | 20 Negative (40%) | 10 Neutral (20%) | 5 Mixed (10%)

Day 2: 80 events analyzed
‚Üí 24 Positive (30%) | 32 Negative (40%) | 16 Neutral (20%) | 8 Mixed (10%)

Insight: Despite different event volumes, sentiment distribution is identical (30/40/20/10). This makes trend comparison meaningful.

Sentiment Categories:

  • Positive: Events likely to boost AI sector sentiment (launches, funding, growth)
  • Negative: Events likely to dampen sentiment (regulations, failures, controversies)
  • Neutral: Informational events without clear directional impact
  • Mixed: Events with both positive and negative implications

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the briefing published?
1:30pm GMT daily, before US markets open at 2:30pm GMT. This gives you time to read analysis before trading begins.
What if I miss the Discord notification?
Just visit the website directly at ai-pulse.aifinto.com. The briefing is always available and updates remain archived.
How far back does the history go?
All briefings are archived and accessible via the Archive link. Sentiment charts show 30-day trends.
Can I see older briefings?
Yes, click "Archive" in the header to browse all past briefings by date.
How accurate are the predictions?
Accuracy is tracked daily and displayed in each briefing. Predictions are logged at 1:30pm (before market opens) and compared to actual market movement at close.
Why use percentages for sentiment instead of counts?
Event volume varies daily (30-80 events). Percentages normalize this variation, making day-to-day trend comparison meaningful. 40% negative sentiment has the same meaning whether it's 20/50 events or 32/80 events.
What markets are tracked for correlation?
NASDAQ Composite, S&P 500, and major AI-related stocks (NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL, META, AMZN, AMD, ARM, TSMC, ASML).
Is this investment advice?
No. AI Pulse provides intelligence and analysis, not investment recommendations. Always do your own research and consult financial advisors before making investment decisions.
